ChemCell runs on single-processor machines or in parallel using message-passing techniques and a spatial-decomposition of the simulation domain. The code is designed to be easy to modify or extend with new functionality. It's distributed as an open source code under the terms of the GPL license and can be downloaded from this site or from SourceForge.

ChemCell is distributed by Steve Plimpton at Sandia National Laboratories, a US Department of Energy (DOE) laboratory. Many people have contributed to ChemCell; they are acknowledged here. Funding for ChemCell development has come primarily from DOE (OASCR, OBER, ASCI, LDRD, Genomes-to-Life) and is acknowledged here.
